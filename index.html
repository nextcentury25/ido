Frequently Asked Questions

Property tokens reflect real-world asset values, which means token prices can fluctuate based on the underlying property value. Our platform uses professional appraisals and market data oracles to provide accurate, up-to-date valuations. The DAO governance system allows token holders to vote on property management decisions, including whether to hold or sell assets if values decrease significantly. This gives investors direct control over their assets, unlike traditional real estate investments.

Team and advisor tokens have a 24-month vesting schedule with a 6-month cliff. This means no team tokens are released for the first 6 months after the token launch, and then they vest linearly over the following 18 months. This structure aligns the team's incentives with long-term project success and demonstrates our commitment to building sustainable value rather than short-term gains.

Next Century uses a multi-chain approach to mitigate blockchain challenges. By supporting both Solana (for high throughput and low fees) and Base Chain (for EVM compatibility), we provide flexibility during network congestion. Additionally, our platform implements layer-2 solutions and batched transactions to optimize gas costs. For property verification and critical operations, we use Phala's Trusted Execution Environment (TEE) to ensure data integrity even during network disruptions.

Each tokenized property is held in a legally compliant Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) structure that connects token ownership to legal property rights. We work with top legal firms specializing in both real estate and blockchain to ensure our structures comply with regulations in each jurisdiction. The platform uses smart contracts to enforce ownership rights while the legal framework provides traditional legal recourse if needed. This dual-layer approach provides maximum security for investors.
